# Evolution Note: 3.5.0

## Repository state reviewed

At 3.4.0, the archive was consistent, but the root still exposed too many folders and many short documents remained split into separate files.

## Reconsideration

The repository should feel like a small artifact, not a filing cabinet. The goal was not to erase layers, but to move related layers under stronger parent folders and merge fragments that were too small to justify separate paths.

## Changes made

- Moved interpretation layers under `archive/`: `archive/git/`, `archive/rfc/`, `archive/exhibits/`, and `archive/future/`.
- Moved reading durability layers under `preservation/`: `preservation/accessibility/` and `preservation/print/`.
- Moved editorial curation under `guide/curation/`.
- Moved machine-readable data under `meta/data/`.
- Merged short Git, exhibit, accessibility, print, and Korean essay fragments into compact parent documents.
- Updated maps, indexes, metadata, inventory, and checksums.

## Result

The root now shows fewer top-level folders while preserving the archive's conceptual layers. The archive remains bilingual and preservation-oriented, but it is less fragmented.
