# Digital Artifacts

Descriptions of common digital objects as if they were found in a small museum.

## The Cursor

A blinking mark showing where the future will be typed.

## The Button

A small rectangle of intention.

When pressed, it tells a system that a human has decided something.

## The Loading Spinner

A symbol of waiting without explanation.

It says: something is happening, or perhaps nothing is happening, but please remain patient.

## The 404 Page

A memorial for a path that no longer leads anywhere.

## The Password Field

A box where memory, secrecy, and frustration meet.

## The Checkbox

A tiny machine for consent, preference, completion, and bureaucracy.

## The Commit Hash

A fingerprint for a moment in a repository's life.

Long enough to be precise.  
Shortened because humans are impatient.

## The README

A sign placed at the entrance of a project.

Sometimes helpful.  
Sometimes forgotten.  
Sometimes the whole point.

## The Terminal Prompt

A small invitation.

It waits without decoration, asking only:

what now?

## The Progress Bar

A visual promise that the waiting has a shape.

## The Archive File

A box inside a box.

Often created to move many things as one thing.

## The Disc

A circle that can hold songs, photos, code, documents, backups, drivers, memories, installers, mistakes, and ghosts.

A physical object that makes data feel local again.
