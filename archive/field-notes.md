# Field Notes from the Present

Short observations from a time when the internet felt both everywhere and strangely temporary.

## Observation 001

People began searching less like they were querying databases and more like they were asking companions.

## Observation 002

Many decisions that once lived in documents now disappear into chat threads.

## Observation 003

Public spaces became larger, but durable public memory became rarer.

## Observation 004

Software became easier to start and harder to fully understand.

## Observation 005

Plain text remained useful because it asked for very little.

## Observation 006

The more things moved into the cloud, the more meaningful local copies began to feel.

## Observation 007

People wanted automation, but also wanted someone or something to remain accountable.

## Observation 008

A file could be trivial and still become important if it was the only surviving explanation.
