# One-Page Present

People talked to machines.

Machines answered in fluent paragraphs.

Work moved through chats, repositories, dashboards, clouds, and terminals.

Many things were saved automatically, but context was still easy to lose.

Plain text felt old and reliable.

The cloud felt convenient and distant.

A disc felt obsolete and strangely intimate.

A public repository could be a product, a library, a profile, a performance, or a small archive.

This one chose the last option.
