# Notes to the Future

If you are reading this much later:

Hello.

This was written in a time when people were still learning how to live with intelligent machines.

We asked them questions.  
We doubted their answers.  
We asked them to check our doubts.  
We used them for work, study, programming, translation, summaries, jokes, and sometimes comfort.

We still typed commands into terminals.

We still argued about tabs and spaces, about programming languages, about text editors, about whether a tool was elegant or merely popular.

We still made private repositories.

We still made unfinished things.

We still felt that many things were not good enough to show.

But sometimes, a small public mark was enough.

## If this became a disc

If this repository reached you as a physical object, then something unusual happened.

A few text files moved from thought, to keyboard, to repository, to storage, to disc, to your hands, to your eyes.

That path is longer than it looks.

## What to know about us

We were noisy.  
We were distracted.  
We were inventive.  
We were anxious.  
We automated things and then worried about automation.  
We built systems too large to understand, then built tools to explain them.  
We saved everything and still lost context.

We wanted faster machines.

We also wanted quieter lives.

## What to keep

Keep the habit of naming things carefully.

Keep some records in plain text.

Keep a few tools simple enough to understand.

Keep asking whether convenience is worth its cost.

Keep something small, just because it is worth preserving.
