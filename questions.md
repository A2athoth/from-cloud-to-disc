# Questions Worth Keeping

Questions are often more durable than answers.

- What makes a record worth preserving?
- Is a public file still public if no one reads it?
- When does a repository become an archive?
- What should remain plain text?
- What does software forget?
- What does software remember too well?
- Is convenience a kind of dependency?
- How much of a person can be inferred from their tools?
- When does automation become delegation?
- What is the difference between a backup and a memory?
- Why do unfinished things feel less worthy of being shown?
- Can a small artifact be complete without being useful?
- What should be easy to delete?
- What should be hard to lose?
- What will look strange about this moment later?
- Which of our tools will become fossils?
- What did we call progress that was actually noise?
- What did we call temporary that became permanent?
- What kind of software deserves attention?
- What kind of software deserves silence?
