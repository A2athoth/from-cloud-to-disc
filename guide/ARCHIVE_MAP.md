# Archive Map

This is the conceptual map. It explains the archive by role, not by every file.

## 1. Entrance

Quick ways to understand the project: `README.md`, `guide/REVIEWER_GUIDE.md`, `guide/READING_PATHS.md`, `guide/FAQ.md`.

## 2. Core archive

The central time-capsule texts: `core/capsule.md`, `core/present.md`, `core/notes-to-the-future.md`, `core/questions.md`, `core/timeline.md`.

## 3. Archive interpretation

Interpretive and future-facing layers now live under `archive/`: `archive/git/`, `archive/exhibits/`, `archive/future/`, and `archive/rfc/`.

## 4. Korean origin

The language layer that preserves where the idea began: `README.ko.md`, `ko/`, and `language/`.

## 5. Physical media

Texts that imagine the repository as a disc or printed packet: `disc/` and `preservation/print/`.

## 6. Stewardship

Documents that keep the archive readable and restrained: `guide/curation/`, `preservation/`, `preservation/accessibility/`, and `context/`.

## 7. Machine-readable support

Structured metadata and integrity files: `meta/`, `meta/data/`, and `CITATION.cff`.

## 8. Change history

The record of how the archive evolved: `CHANGELOG.md`, `VERSION`, and `evolution/`.
