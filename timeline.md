# Timeline

## Before

Most repositories were private, practical, unfinished, or not meant for public reading.

## Moment

A public repository became necessary for an event connected to compact discs.

Instead of pretending to have a conventional project, the repository became a record about the situation itself.

## After

The archive grew into a bilingual, plain-text time capsule with disc, preservation, semantic, and language layers.

## Later

The repository may be forgotten, selected, ignored, copied, printed, burned to disc, or rediscovered.

All of those outcomes fit the artifact.
